Future Cityscapes: Visualizing the Family Zoning Plan

Updates
  • Aug 11, 2025. First release .

Overview

SF's proposed rezoning will not have an overnight impact: instead, development occurs slowly. This is an attempt to visualize the long-term impacts of the currently proposed Family Zoning Plan.

These views simulate a potential view of the city in 10 years, under the "Family Zoning" proposal, based on our best understanding of how much development is likely. These images are not meant to be exact, but rather to give a sense of how the city might look in the future.

Process

We simulated the 10-year future of SF under the "Family Zoning" proposal.
  1. Used the open-sourced economic model published by RezoneSF ShinyApp
  2. Applied a correction factor to align development probabilities with SF Planning's forecast
  3. For each site, for each year, simulate development outcomes
  4. Gather the list of "developed" sites over the simulation period
  5. Estimate their height as a random number between 70-100% of their zoned height limit
  6. Compute their allowable lot coverage based on parcel geometry
  7. Create a 3D rendering of a potential building in Blender/Rhino (note: this likely overestimates bulk, as not all objective design standards are integrated)
  8. Import into Google Earth
  9. Toggle the visibility of the new on/off for a "before" and "after" photos
